PERFORMERS

Aura Walmer

Aura Walmer is an information designer, researcher, artist, and audio enthusiast. She uses interdisciplinary methods to combine data, art, and sound for the purpose of communication and learning. She currently works for University of Michigan's Institute for Social Research, and recently completed a journalism fellowship to build an open-source data sonification toolkit: https://www.sonificationkit.com/. Her data-driven projects and sound design work can be found on her website, at www.aurawalmer.com.

Jordan Wirfs-Brock

Jordan Wirfs-Brock is an assistant professor of computer science at Whitman College whose research explores how we might develop sonic archives of personally meaningful everyday sounds. Her practice sits at the intersection of data, design, and storytelling, examining how sound can bridge human experience and computation. Through projects like Your Life in Sound and the Resonate Data Lab, she investigates how listening ‚Äî both human and machine ‚Äî can become a creative and critical act for understanding data and ourselves.

Tristan Peng

Tristan Peng is a Music Technology PhD student at the Georgia Institute of Technology exploring interaction design, spatial audio, and sonification. A creative technologist and musician, his work aims to democratize music through technology -- creating accessible, artful, and interactive ways for people to experience sound. His current projects investigate how data can become a medium for participation and how immersive audio spaces can evoke emotion and understanding in ways that traditional visualizations cannot.
